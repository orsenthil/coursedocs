Short Selling
=============

* Sell borrowed stock; buy back later to return to broker
* **Initial margin** = % of contract value deposited at entry
* **Maintenance margin** = minimum balance broker requires at all times
* Futures sold in **lots** only
* Exit: **Buy to exit** short | **Sell to exit** long


Trend Lines
===========

**Purpose**

* Join **lows** in uptrend, **peaks** in downtrend
* Confirms trend; trade **with** trend (counter-trend = high risk)
* Shows where **retracement/profit booking** should stop
* **Breakout** (price crosses trend line) → new trend (reversal, continuation, or sideways)
* After breakout, price often **retests** trend line and fails → new trend established

**Rules**

* Uptrend line = **support** (positive slope → rising demand)
* Downtrend line = **resistance** (negative slope → rising supply)
* Break **below** uptrend line → demand weakness
* Break **above** downtrend line → demand exceeds supply
* Above trend line → **buy only**; below → **sell only**

**Drawing accurately**

* Use **log scale** on Y-axis for long-term charts (percentage moves, not absolute $)
* Ignore **spikes**; connect meaningful swing points
* Positional trading: draw on **daily** chart, **2–4 year** look-back
* Draw **main** trend line + optional **internal** trend line for signals


Basic Terms & Platform
======================

**TradingView tools**: candlestick, line, Heikin-Ashi, Fibonacci retracement, S&R zones, H&S, XABCD, harmonic patterns (bat, crab, butterfly, cypher), RSI, SMA, EMA

**Demand & Supply**

* **Support** = demand strong enough to halt decline (price **zone**, not exact level)
* **Resistance** = supply strong enough to halt rise (price **zone**)
* Speed of price into zone indicates strength of level

**Dynamic S&R**: moving averages, price channels, Fibonacci (e.g. **61.8%** level as support)


Volume
======

* Shares traded in a period; **independent of price**
* Long bar vs average = **high relative volume** (unusual activity)
* Every buyer has a seller (and vice versa)
* **Smart money** trades in millions → huge volume at **extremes** of trends
* Volume at **resistance breakout** → continuation more likely


Candlestick Patterns
====================

**Anatomy**

* **Green/bullish**: close > open | **Red/bearish**: open > close
* **Real body** + **wicks/shadows** (high/low)

.. list-table::
   :header-rows: 1
   :widths: 20 35 45

   * - Pattern
     - Structure
     - Rules
   * - **Hammer**
     - Small body top, lower wick ≥ **2× body**; in **downtrend**
     - Reversal up; confirm: next close **above** hammer close; SL below support/low
   * - **Hanging Man**
     - Same shape; in **uptrend**
     - Reversal down; confirm: next close **below** hanging man close; SL above resistance
   * - **Shooting Star**
     - Small body bottom, upper wick ≥ **2× body**; at **resistance**
     - Reversal down; confirm: next close below; green candle before = stronger signal
   * - **Doji**
     - Open ≈ close
     - **Indecision**; reversal near S&R
   * - **Dragonfly Doji**
     - Open = high = close, long lower shadow
     - Bullish reversal (like hammer)
   * - **Gravestone Doji**
     - Open = high = close, long upper shadow
     - Bearish reversal (like shooting star)
   * - **Marubozu**
     - Long body, **no/minimal wicks** — **momentum** candle (not reversal)
     - Green = strong bulls; Red = strong bears; high **volume** confirms
   * - **Bearish Engulfing**
     - Red candle fully engulfs prior candle at S&R after sharp move
     - Pullback/reversal signal
   * - **Bullish Engulfing**
     - Green candle fully engulfs prior red at support
     - Uptrend after downtrend correction

**Color notes**: green hammer / red hanging man = higher significance

**Critical rule**: never trust patterns alone — must be at **S&R** + confirmed by **other indicators**

Support & Resistance
======================

**Drawing**

* Use **higher timeframe** from 3D setup (e.g. weekly for positional)
* Horizontal line joining swing **highs** (resistance) or **lows** (support)
* Minimum **2 touches**; more touches = **stronger** level
* Adjust on lower TF using wick precision
* **Role reversal**: broken support → resistance (and vice versa)
* Two nearby levels = **S/R zone** (stronger than single level)

Technical Indicators — Overview
===============================

**Two categories**

* **Leading indicators** — lead price; early entry/exit; e.g. oscillators
* **Lagging indicators** — trend-following; catch and stay in moves; signal late; poor in sideways markets

**Principles**

* Use a **small selected set** — too many = confusion
* No single indicator works in all conditions
* Combine indicators of **different nature** (not RSI + Stochastic together)


Lagging Indicators — Moving Averages
====================================

**Types**

* **SMA** — simple average; use on **large TFs** (weekly/monthly)
* **EMA** — weights recent prices; use on **small TFs** (hourly/15 min); reacts faster

**200-day MA rule**

* **Bulls live above** 200 MA | **Bears live below** 200 MA
* Acts as dynamic **support** (uptrend) or **resistance** (downtrend)

**Period sets**

.. list-table::
   :header-rows: 1

   * - Trader
     - MA Periods
   * - Long-term investor
     - **200**, **100**, **50** day
   * - Positional
     - **50**, **21**, **14**
   * - Intraday
     - **14, 7, 3 EMA** or **20, 10, 5**

**Crossover signals**

* Fast MA crosses **above** slow MA → **buy**
* Fast MA crosses **below** slow MA → **sell/exit**
* Positional example: **14 EMA** + **21 EMA** for signals; **50 MA** as trend filter
  * Longs only when price + fast MAs **above 50 MA**
  * Shorts only when fast MAs **below 50 MA**

**Rules**

* Use only in **trending** markets (not choppy/ranging)
* Check **monthly/weekly** first for bigger picture
* Combine with **leading** indicators for confirmation

MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)
============================================

**Settings**: default **12 EMA**, **26 EMA**, **9** signal line

**Components**

* **MACD line** (blue) = 12 EMA − 26 EMA
* **Signal line** (orange)
* **Histogram** (green/red bars)
* **Zero line** (center)

**Trend read**

* MACD **above zero** → bullish | **below zero** → bearish

**Signals**

* **Buy**: MACD crosses **above** signal line, both **above zero**, histogram rising/diverging
* **Exit long**: lines **converging**, histogram falling
* **Short**: bearish crossover + both lines **below zero** (retracement in uptrend) or confirmed downtrend
* **Exit short**: lines converging

**Rules**

* Accurate in **trending** markets only; poor in ranging
* **Never short** in uptrend if MACD still above zero
* **Never counter-trend** as beginner
* Determine trend on **middle TF**; take signals on **lower TF**
* Always combine with 3–4 indicators


Oscillators — RSI
=================

* `RSI`_

**Basics**

* Momentum oscillator; range **0–100**; default **14-day**
* Key levels: **70, 60, 50, 40, 30**

**Overbought / Oversold**

* RSI **> 70** → overbought | RSI **< 30** → oversold
* RSI **> 50** → bullish bias | RSI **< 50** → bearish bias

**Trending vs Ranging — CRITICAL**

.. list-table::
   :header-rows: 1

   * - Market
     - RSI Behavior
     - Action
   * - **Ranging**
     - Buy near **30/40** support; sell near **70/60** resistance
     - Standard OB/OS rules apply
   * - **Trending (bull)**
     - RSI can stay **> 70** for extended periods ("5th gear" momentum)
     - **Do NOT short** overbought; hold long until RSI breaks **below 70**
   * - **Trending (bear)**
     - RSI can stay **< 30** for extended periods
     - **Do NOT buy** oversold blindly; bottoms can go much lower

**Entry/Exit rules**

* Buy: RSI breaks **above 30**, confirm when crosses **above 50**
* Exit long: RSI breaks **below 70** after being overbought
* Short: RSI breaks **below 50** in downtrend; exit when RSI rises **above 30**
* **Divergence** between RSI and price → early reversal signal

.. _RSI: https://att-c.udemycdn.com/2019-12-22_05-36-48-585680b3085aa66ccfebfde96e586568/original.pdf


Oscillators — Stochastic
========================

* `Stochastics`_

**Settings**: **K = 14**, **D = 3** (D = MA of K); range **0–100**

**Key levels**: **20** (oversold), **80** (overbought), **50** (bull/bear mode)

**Signals**

* **Buy**: K crosses **above** D, both cross **above 20** (or rise from below 20)
* **Sell**: K crosses **below** D, both cross **below 80**
* K & D **above 50** → bullish mode | **below 50** → bearish mode

**Pattern read**

* **Narrow bottom + broad top** → bears weak, bulls strong (uptrend)
* **Narrow top + broad bottom** → bulls weak, bears strong (downtrend)

**Usage rules**

* Accurate on **short TF / ranging** markets; **fails in strong trends**
* **Do NOT use with RSI** — similar nature; pick one
* Best combined with indicators of **different nature** (e.g. MA + Stochastic)
* In range: buy at support + oversold signal; sell at resistance + overbought signal

.. _Stochastics: https://att-c.udemycdn.com/2019-12-22_05-36-48-6aac528fb0beec63e48611ca6e71e713/original.pdf


Leading Indicators
==================

**Leading indicators** predict future trend direction (vs lagging indicators that confirm after the move).

Divergence
----------

**Definition:** Mismatch between **price** and an **oscillator** (RSI, MACD, Stochastic). A **leading** signal — often used for **early exit**, not primary entry.

.. list-table::
   :header-rows: 1
   :widths: 20 40 40

   * - Type
     - Price action
     - Indicator action
   * - **Bullish (positive) divergence**
     - Lower lows (downtrend)
     - Higher lows / upward (e.g. RSI troughs rising)
   * - **Bearish (negative) divergence**
     - Higher highs (uptrend)
     - Lower highs / downward (e.g. RSI peaks falling)

**How to read**

- **Uptrend:** compare **RSI peaks** (join peaks on price vs RSI — lines diverge).
- **Downtrend:** compare **RSI troughs/lows**.
- Prefer **RSI** (also usable on MACD, Stochastic); RSI preferred for pullback entry signals.
- **Primary use:** early signal to **exit** long (bearish div near resistance) or **exit** short (bullish div near support). Counter-trend entry possible but **riskier** — confirm first.

**Validity rules (critical)**

- Divergence is **invalid** if the oscillator is in the **middle range** (RSI between 30–70).
- **Bearish divergence:** RSI peaks must be in **overbought** zone; **first peak must be > 70**.
- **Bullish divergence:** RSI troughs must be in **oversold** zone (**< 30**).

**Caveats**

- Reversal after divergence can be **delayed** — patience + confirmation required before entry/exit.
- Advanced types (failing, multiple, simple divergence) covered in later material.

Bollinger Bands
---------------

**Inventor:** John Bollinger (1980s). **Purpose:** relative definition of high/low; **volatility** indicator.

**Construction (default settings)**

.. list-table::
   :header-rows: 1
   :widths: 30 70

   * - Component
     - Setting
   * - Middle band
     - **20-period SMA**
   * - Upper band
     - **+2 standard deviations** above middle
   * - Lower band
     - **−2 standard deviations** below middle

Use default settings unless strong reason to change.

**Three main uses**

1. **Volatility gauge**
   - **Bands expanding** → high volatility; expect sharp moves.
   - **Bands contracting / narrow** → low volatility; prices **range-bound / sideways**.
   - **Rule:** do **not** trade on the higher TF while bands are sideways/contracting.

2. **Breakout warning**
   - Extreme **contraction** → probability of **breakout** increases; watch for **expansion** after squeeze.

3. **Trend continuation**
   - After contraction, **candle closes outside bands** → momentum high; trend may **continue**.
   - **Candle entirely outside band** (body fully beyond band) → **pullback** signal (not necessarily full reversal).
   - Price riding **upper band** → uptrend continuation; riding **lower band** → downtrend continuation.

**Trading rules**

- **Avoid** buying upper band / selling lower band in a **sideways** Bollinger range on the same TF — stop-loss risk is high.
- **Lower TF workaround:** if hourly is range-bound, trade the range on **15-min or 5-min** with other indicators.
- **Exit** when bands **contract during a trend** (upper band falling + lower band rising) — volatility decreasing; sideways move likely.
- **Combine** with RSI, moving averages, and **support/resistance** to filter fake breakouts (e.g. head-and-shoulders at resistance).
- Price **failing to reach** the band + RSI divergence → decreasing momentum/volatility.

**Classification:** leading indicator (hourly signals).

**Role:** support/resistance tool — does **not** identify trend; predicts **where** retracements and extensions may stall. Most useful after a **clear up-move or down-move**.

Fibonacci Theory
----------------

**Origin:** Leonardo Fibonacci — ratios from the sequence **1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13, 21, …** (each term = sum of prior two).

**Key ratios**

.. list-table::
   :header-rows: 1
   :widths: 35 35 30

   * - Ratio
     - Derivation
     - Role
   * - **1.618**
     - Later ÷ earlier term in sequence (→ 1.618 as terms grow)
     - **Golden ratio**
   * - **0.618**
     - Inverse of 1.618
     - **Golden mean**; major retracement
   * - **0.382**
     - 1 − 0.618
     - Retracement level
   * - **0.236**
     - 0.618 − 0.382
     - Retracement level
   * - **0.5**
     - Half of 1
     - Retracement level
   * - **0.786**
     - √0.618
     - Retracement level
   * - **1.272**
     - √1.618
     - **Extension** level
   * - **1.618**
     - Golden ratio
     - **Extension** level (target/resistance)
   * - **2.618**
     - 1.618 × 2 (approx.)
     - **Extension** level

**Retracement levels:** 0.236, 0.382, 0.5, 0.618, 0.786 — watched globally; act as **S/R** where corrections often pause.

**Extension levels:** 1.272, 1.618, 2.618 — used for **profit targets**; price often resists at these on the next leg.

Fibonacci Retracement and Extensions
------------------------------------

**Retracement tool — draw direction**

.. list-table::
   :header-rows: 1
   :widths: 25 35 40

   * - Trend
     - Draw from → to
     - Levels act as
   * - **Uptrend**
     - **Low → high** (current swing)
     - **Support** on pullback
   * - **Downtrend**
     - **High → low** (current swing)
     - **Resistance** on pullback rally

**Extension tool — reverse the anchors** (same tool, opposite direction)

.. list-table::
   :header-rows: 1
   :widths: 25 35 40

   * - Trend
     - Draw from → to
     - Levels act as
   * - **Uptrend**
     - **High → low**
     - **Target / resistance** above
   * - **Downtrend**
     - **Low → high**
     - **Target / support** below

**Entry / exit logic**

- **Buy** at retracement support (uptrend): stop-loss **below next lower** Fib level.
- **Short** at retracement resistance (downtrend): stop-loss **slightly above** the level / candle high.
- If level **breaks**, next Fib level becomes the new S/R.
- **Extension targets:** after entry at e.g. 38.2% support, set targets at 1.272, 1.618, etc.; breached resistance often becomes support on retest.

**Three practical problems**

1. **Unknown which level holds** — retracements vary (38.2%, 50%, 78.6% all common); add **MA, Bollinger, MACD, RSI** for confirmation.
2. **Trend may not resume** after support — always use stop-loss below entry level.
3. **Which swing to measure** — many nested swings; pick the **current swing on the higher TF** (weekly → daily → hourly).

Fibonacci Practical
-------------------

**Multi-timeframe workflow (positional trading)**

.. list-table::
   :header-rows: 1
   :widths: 20 40 40

   * - Timeframe
     - Purpose
     - Action
   * - **Weekly**
     - Trend + retracement context
     - Apply Fib **low → high** (uptrend); mark levels **near current price**
   * - **Daily**
     - Intermediate swing
     - Retracement: **high → low** (downtrend correction); extension: **reverse** tool for targets
   * - **Hourly**
     - Entry/exit timing
     - Add **MA set (50, 21, 14)** + **RSI**; trade signals at Fib S/R

**Example pattern (downtrend correction)**

- Weekly: stock in uptrend retracement → mark 23.6%, 38.2%, 50% zone.
- Daily: first swing retraces to **38.2%** → reverse tool for **1.272 extension** target; next swing pullback to **61.8%** (golden ratio) → consolidate between levels.
- Hourly: **bullish RSI divergence** at extension support + MA buy signal → long with stop below support candle low; exit on MA/RSI sell signals or negative divergence.

**Key reminders**

- Mark only levels **relevant to current price action**.
- Fib levels + **divergence** at major weekly/daily support → high-probability long entry.
- When next Fib level is **very close** to entry, reduce size or wait for clearer price action.
- Resistance broken → often becomes support on retest (and vice versa).


Chart Pattern Analysis
======================

Gap Theory
----------

**Gap** — unfilled area (no trades) between close and next open. **Up gap**: open > prior close; **down gap**: open < prior close. Filled gaps often reverse then continue in gap direction.

+--------------+----------------------------------+----------------------------------------+
| Type         | Context                          | Rule                                   |
+==============+==================================+========================================+
| **Common**   | Sideways range; filled in days   | Not trade-worthy                       |
+--------------+----------------------------------+----------------------------------------+
| **Breakaway**| Congestion breakout; high volume | **Buy** up / **sell** down; breakout → |
|              |                                  | support (up) or resistance (down)      |
+--------------+----------------------------------+----------------------------------------+
| **Runaway**  | Mid-trend; 2–3× volume; aka      | Rarely filled; **measuring gap** —     |
| (continuation| **measuring/continuation gap**   | target = trend-start-to-gap distance   |
| / measuring) |                                  | projected from gap                     |
+--------------+----------------------------------+----------------------------------------+
| **Exhaustion**| End of move; low vol into gap,  | Filled fast; trend reverses; no new HH |
|              | vol spike; no new HH/LL          | (up) or LL (down)                      |
+--------------+----------------------------------+----------------------------------------+

**Island reversal** — gap opposite exhaustion gap leaving an island; strong reversal confirm.

Reversal Patterns
-----------------

**Head & Shoulders (top)** — left shoulder → head (higher peak) → right shoulder (peak **below** head). **Neckline** = support line through lows. **Entry**: short neckline breakdown. **Stop**: right-shoulder peak. High volume confirms. Multiple shoulders OK; upsloping neckline OK if right-shoulder low < left-shoulder top.

**Inverted H&S** — mirror in downtrend. **Entry**: buy neckline breakout (**daily close above**). **Stop**: below head (head low < both shoulders). Breakout volume > average.

**Rising wedge** — bearish; HH + HL, wide bottom → narrow top. **Entry**: short lower trendline break + **negative RSI divergence**.

**Falling wedge** — bullish reversal in downtrend. **Entry**: buy upper trendline break + **positive RSI divergence** (wait for confirm; fakes possible).

**Double bottom** — W at support, retest resistance, breakout. **Entry**: buy resistance break. **Stop**: low of second bottom.

**Double top** — M; two peaks same level; **3–4 week** min between peaks; vol on 2nd peak < 1st. **Entry**: short neckline break. **Stop**: second peak high. Don't assume until neckline breached; close peaks = consolidation.

**Broadening triangle** — bearish top; 3–4 higher highs + lower lows. **Entry**: short lower trendline break. **Stop**: last peak high.

**Rounding bottom** — long-term U/saucer (weekly clearer). **Entry**: buy near curve end.

Continuation Patterns
---------------------

Pause → breakout resumes prior trend.

+----------------------------+------------------------------------------------+
| Pattern                    | Entry                                          |
+============================+================================================+
| **Symmetrical triangle**   | HH/LL converge; vol fades then spikes. Trade   |
|                            | breakout direction + heavy volume.             |
+----------------------------+------------------------------------------------+
| **Ascending triangle**     | Flat top + rising lows. **Buy** top break + vol.|
+----------------------------+------------------------------------------------+
| **Descending triangle**    | Flat bottom + lower highs. **Short** bottom    |
|                            | break + vol (ignore bullish break).            |
+----------------------------+------------------------------------------------+
| **Flag**                   | Up trend: flag slopes down → **buy** upper     |
|                            | break. Down trend: flag up → **short** lower  |
|                            | break. High volume.                            |
+----------------------------+------------------------------------------------+
| **Rectangle**              | Horizontal flag; range-trade or breakout.      |
+----------------------------+------------------------------------------------+
| **Cup & handle**           | Cup + handle (often falling wedge). **Buy**    |
|                            | handle break + rising volume.                  |
+----------------------------+------------------------------------------------+
| **Falling wedge** (cont.)  | In uptrend only. **Buy** upper break.          |
+----------------------------+------------------------------------------------+
| **Rising wedge** (cont.)   | In downtrend only. **Short** lower break.      |
+----------------------------+------------------------------------------------+

**Wedge rule**: falling wedge — downtrend = reversal, uptrend = continuation. Rising wedge — uptrend = reversal, downtrend = continuation. Reversal wedges need RSI divergence; continuation wedges don't.

Risk Management — Stop Loss
=============================

**Primary SL** — fixed below entry (long) / above (short): support/resistance break, Fib, trendline, MA, or entry-candle low/high (e.g. below 200 MA).

**Trailing SL** — locks profit via faster MA below slower (7/14, 8/20: exit when fast crosses under slow), trendline, % profit, or RSI (exit long when RSI drops below 70).

Intraday example: buy 50% Fib cross; primary SL 61.8%; trail slowest short MA; exit on fast/slow cross or trendline rejection.

Paper Trading & Order Types
===========================

**Paper trading** — TradingView simulation with fake money.

+---------------+----------------------+------------------------------------------+
| Order         | vs market            | Use                                      |
+===============+======================+==========================================+
| **Market**    | Current price        | Immediate fill; attach SL/target.        |
+---------------+----------------------+------------------------------------------+
| **Limit**     | Buy below / sell above| Discount/premium entry; fill at limit.  |
+---------------+----------------------+------------------------------------------+
| **Stop**      | Buy above / sell below| Breakout above resistance / breakdown   |
|               |                      | below support; SL/target after fill.     |
+---------------+----------------------+------------------------------------------+

Scale out; move SL to breakeven. Use position tool for R:R (min **1:1**, target **1:2**).

Common Mistakes
---------------

- Trade only on **confirmed signals** — analyze, don't react to noise or FOMO mid-trend.
- Don't **fear-exit** without analysis; don't **overtrade** from boredom.
- Min **1:1 R:R**; avoid big risk for small reward.
- Control greed/regret (holding for "deserved" profit or chasing past $200 wins).
- No permanent bull/bear bias — adapt to trend; buy support on triggers in uptrends.

Advanced Concepts
=================

Accumulation / Distribution
---------------------------

* **Leading** vol/price divergence indicator.
* **+div** (price ↓, A/D ↑): buy trendline break, SL = signal candle low.
* **−div** (price ↑/flat, A/D ↓): sell break, SL above resistance.

Stochastic RSI
--------------

* Stochastic on **RSI**. **Default 14,14,3,3** · OB **>80** · OS **<20**.
* Hourly for short-term; trade **with intermediate trend**.
* On 15m/1h minor trends: **combine with Chaikin** (volume-based).

Chaikin Oscillator
------------------

* **3-day EMA(A/D) − 10-day EMA(A/D)**.
* Long: +div + cross **above 0** · Short: −div + cross **below 0**.
* **+ Stoch RSI:** buy Chaikin +div + Stoch RSI OS; exit Stoch RSI OB/sell. Never alone; no counter-trend.

VWMA vs SMA
-----------

.. list-table::
   :header-rows: 1
   :widths: 40 60

   * - Setup
     - Meaning
   * - VWMA between price & SMA; VWMA > SMA
     - Bullish
   * - Price < VWMA; SMA > VWMA
     - Bearish
   * - SMA between price & VWMA
     - Reversal signal
   * - VWMA/SMA gap widens then overlaps
     - Trend ending → **liquidate**
   * - VWMA −div
     - Downtrend warning

Parabolic SAR
-------------

* Dots below = buy · above = sell · flip = reversal.
* Wide dot gap = strong trend; tight = chop (**false signals**).
* Pair with **8/21 MA** or MACD; also **SMI Ergodic**. Dynamic S/R + trailing stop.

SMI Ergodic
-----------

* Faster filtered signals vs MACD. **Default 20,5,10**.
* Buy: blue > orange · Exit: blue crosses below orange.
* Trade **intermediate trend**; with PSAR only when both agree; add Chaikin for confidence.

Super Trend
-----------

* **ATR 7, multiplier 3–4**. Green below = buy; red above = sell.
* Dynamic S/R + trailing stop; dip to line = re-entry in trend direction.
* Pair with **RSI**; check **Choppiness** first.

Choppiness Index
----------------

* **Period 40** · **>61.8%** = choppy · **<38.2%** = trending.
* Buy/sell on break below 38.2%; exit above 61.8%.
* 61.8% hit **2–3×** then 38.2% = strong trend.

ADX
---

* **ADX, +DI, −DI**. ADX **>20** = trending · **<20** = range.
* **+DI > −DI** = buy pressure · reverse for sell.
* Rising ADX = strengthening; falling = weakening (not always reversing). Cross above 20 = breakout.

Multi-Collinearity
------------------

* Don't stack same-category indicators (e.g. RSI + Stoch + CCI).
* **Categories:** Momentum · Trend-following · Volume/volatility · Chart studies.
* **Good combo:** RSI + ADX + SuperTrend. Use **3–4 complementary** indicators.

Indicator Settings — Multiple Timeframes
----------------------------------------

#. Define perspective → select **3D set**.
#. **Higher TF:** S/R + Fib on last swing.
#. **Middle TF:** Trend, patterns, trendlines (**log scale**), divergences, indicators.
#. **Lower TF:** Entry/exit via MA crossovers.

.. list-table::
   :header-rows: 1
   :widths: 22 28 50

   * - Perspective
     - 3D Set
     - Middle-TF / Lower-TF
   * - Long-term investor
     - Daily / Weekly / Monthly
     - RSI, ADX, Chaikin, Fib · Lower: **200/100/50 EMA** — hold 50>100
   * - Short-term
     - Hourly / Daily / Weekly
     - RSI, Chaikin, MACD, Fib · Lower: RSI (trend) or Stoch RSI (range)
   * - Day trader
     - 5m / 15m / 60m
     - RSI, MACD, Chaikin · Lower: **14/7/3** or **20/10/5 EMA** + RSI + Bollinger

**Fib rules:** Uptrend below peak → low-to-high retracement; above peak → high-to-low extension. Downtrend: mirror (above bottom = retracement; below bottom = extension).
