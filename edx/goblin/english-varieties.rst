Australian English
------------------

- **Macquarie Dictionary** gives legitimacy to Australian vocabulary and distinctions.
- **Descriptivist** approach: note how language is actually used rather than prescribing rigid rules.
- *Between you and me* (not *I*) — object of preposition *between* requires *me*.

Reference: `What Even is Australian English? <http://junkee.com/what-even-is-australian-english-an-interview-with-the-editor-of-the-macquarie-dictionary/39351>`_

Youse
^^^^^

Australian plural of **you** (more than one person). Included in the Macquarie Dictionary despite controversy.

Reference: `Guardian — Australia's national dictionary editor on the heat about youse <http://www.theguardian.com/books/australia-culture-blog/2014/aug/12/australias-national-dictionary-editor-on-the-heat-about-youse>`_


British and American Spelling
-----------------------------

British English often retains spellings from borrowed languages; American English tends toward simplified forms.

.. list-table::
   :header-rows: 1

   * - American
     - British
   * - color
     - colour
   * - neighbor
     - neighbour
   * - catalog
     - catalogue
   * - center
     - centre
   * - organize
     - organise

Reference: `Oxford Dictionaries — British and American spelling <http://www.oxforddictionaries.com/words/british-and-american-spelling>`_
